Ingénieur d'Études at Alten (Rennes, France)

Alten Rennes's Software Developmet Center

The S.D.C. was a division of Alten Rennes, which was aimed to provide a comptetive service, compared to Alcatel's indian subsidiaries. The argument was :

  • Same langage (French) for a better communication
  • Geographic Proximty (Rennes to Nantes)
  • Talented but cheap engineering team, by employing mainly young people, newly graduated
  • Reducing overall project cost and shrinking delays by improved efficiency

Context

Open Service Platform of Alcatel is a multi-blade server technology, powered by Solaris. Its role is to manage the classical commutated telephone network, to implement what is called the Intelligent Network. It allow valuable services far way beyond reaching the receipient of a voice call (which is the basic task of PTSN and does not imply I.N.). For instance, Flexible Routing and Charging (FRC) service allows a victim of a break down on the motorway, to call a special number (nationally unique) from its mobile and reach the nearest <b><YourBrand></b> Automobile Technical Center, that is open, and having room for this customer. A rescue vehicule is alread gone !

ECN was another Intelligent Network service, dedicated at first to companies that spread around multiple locations but wanted to take advantage of the benefits of on-site PABX, like short numbers to reach another corporate, spreading across all site of the corporation. It now allows fixe/mobile convergence (corporate people can seamlessly initiate a short-number call from their mobile phone, off-site : this is because the service can be triggered as soon as the user pick up the phone ! It also allows the corporate user to manage its own mobility, setup call routing (e.g. to office, mobile, home or voicemail) from a public web interface (obv. using authentication).
